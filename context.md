# Current Research

## What we are exploring

Three threads against NVIDIA's Nemotron model family:

1. **Training patterns** — How Nemotron trains models: recipes, pipelines, pruning, distillation, optimization techniques. What's transferable to smaller-scale training work (nanochat, personal models, single-subject fine-tuning).

2. **Agentic AI architecture** — How Nemotron models are designed for agentic workflows: tool calling, RAG pipelines, instruction following, deployment across edge/GPU/datacenter tiers. What patterns apply to building AI systems where the model is a component, not the product.

3. **Open exploration** — Unexpected connections and ideas that emerge from reading the source material. Cross-pollination between Nemotron's approach and other projects, frameworks, or thinking.

## What this repo contains

NVIDIA Nemotron is a family of open, high-efficiency multimodal models for agentic AI:

- **Model tiers**: Nano (edge/PC), Super (single GPU), Ultra (multi-GPU datacenter)
- **Training recipes** (`src/nemotron/recipes/`): Complete reproducible pipelines — pruning, distillation, SFT, alignment, evaluation
- **Usage cookbooks** (`usage-cookbook/`): Deployment guides, inference notebooks, TensorRT-LLM, vLLM, NIM
- **Use-case examples** (`use-case-examples/`): RAG, agents, tool use, agentic workflows

## What good looks like

- Synthesized ideas that are conceptual and transferable — not summaries of what the repo contains
- New connections between Nemotron's patterns and our own projects and thinking
- Insights about training methodology that inform how we approach model training at any scale
- Architectural patterns from agentic workflows that transfer to our own AI system design
- Surprises — things we didn't expect to find or connections we didn't expect to make

## What to avoid

- Summarizing without synthesizing — if it could be generated by reading the README, it's not a finding
- Treating findings as implementation plans — these are conceptual, not actionable specs
- Modifying upstream code — this is a read-only research reference
- Ignoring the open thread — the most valuable findings are often the ones we weren't looking for
